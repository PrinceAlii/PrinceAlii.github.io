## Design Context

### Users
Primary users are recruiters and hiring managers evaluating candidate fit quickly. Secondary users include technical peers and collaborators who want deeper implementation detail.

Users typically scan this site in short sessions, often while comparing multiple candidates. The primary job to be done is to establish credibility fast through clear experience, practical project evidence, and direct contact paths.

### Brand Personality
The brand should feel: confident, aesthetic, and intelligent.

Voice and tone should be concise, technical, and grounded in substance over hype. The interface should create a recruiter-relevant feeling of confidence in capability and professional maturity.

### Aesthetic Direction
Keep the current terminal-inspired, engineering-forward visual language, but ensure it remains deliberate and polished rather than generic portfolio styling.

Avoid patterns that look like "AI slop" or templated AI portfolios. Use strong visual hierarchy, clean spacing rhythm, and restrained but intentional accents.

Color direction should explicitly avoid purple and pink hues.

Component-level note: preserve logo legibility and edge contrast (for example, reduce the ABS logo footprint slightly so white logo edges do not visually merge with white container borders).

### Design Principles
1. Optimize for recruiter decision speed.
2. Show evidence over claims.
3. Be distinctive without being noisy.
4. Maintain visual precision and consistency.
5. Prioritize practical accessibility and readable content flow.
